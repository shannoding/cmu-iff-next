Abendland *  (Austria, 2011)
Wednesday, April 10, 5:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


*A Goodman Faces of Work Film

Click here for more information. 
Abendland  is a nocturnal journey through a world that never sleeps.  This riveting documentary presents scenes of European nightlife in a seamless montage, capturing everything from a hospital ward, to a porn-shoot, to an airport bathroom.  Surveillance and machine technology bring together a variety of seemingly unrelated situations in a fragmented Europe.  The film is a self-reflexive collage examining the complex ways in which media communications help to ensure national security.

Followed by a discussion moderated by Neepa Majumdar, Associate Professor of English and Film Studies at Pitt! 

You’ve Been Trumped *  (UK, 2011)
Wednesday, April 10, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


*A Goodman Faces of Work Film

Click here for more information. 
A group of proud and determined Scottish homeowners take on celebrity tycoon Donald Trump, who plans to build a luxury hotel and golf complex on one of Scotland's last stretches of wilderness. This film explores the controversy that arose and the 21st century David and Goliath battle that ensued. You've Been Trumped is an entertaining, inspiring, and occasionally disturbing parable of our times and values.

A screening of Dr. Paul Goodman’s short film University President will precede the feature film! There will also be a discussion moderated by Roger Rouse, Professor of History at CMU, and a reception courtesy of East End Food Co-Op.

The Punk Syndrome  (Finland, 2012)
Thursday, April 11, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information. 
The Punk Syndrome is a thrilling and graceful documentary about Perti Kurikka's Name Day, a group of four punk rockers - each with a mystery or a secret to him - currently climbing its way to popularity in Finland.  Filmed amidst practice jams, tours, and an upcoming album release, the film captures the band's passion for honest punk music in an alternative culture of "misfits" that desires real social change. 

A performance by Pittsburgh punk rock band Brightside and a reception provided by La Gourmandine will follow the screening!

The Ballad of Genesis and Lady Jaye (USA/France, 2011)
Friday, April 12, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information. 
The Ballad of Genesis and Lady Jaye is a gorgeously shot film that glimpses into the lives of Genesis, an innovative English rocker and widely influential avant-garde performance artist, and h/er love, Lady Jaye Breyer P-Orridge. Director Marie Losier provides a heartfelt look into the couple's "Pandrogyne" project, an artistic experiment that defied biology and challenged preconceived notions of love and partnership.

Director Marie Losier will introduce her film, and Melissa Ragona, Professor of Art at CMU, will moderate a discussion after the screening! There will also be a performance by Harrison Apple, Dani Lamorte, and Stephanie Ross, and a reception!

Back to the Square  (Norway/Canada, 2012)  -  Closing Night!
Saturday, April 13, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information. 
Filmed throughout the first year of the Egyptian Revolution's aftermath, Back to the Square is a powerful documentary that reveals the Egyptian citizens' continuing struggles against injustices at the hands of the State.  Director Petr Lom explores the lives of five seemingly unrelated Egyptians and addresses the extensive human rights issues still plaguing their nation.

Features a discussion with director Petr Lom and a Closing Night reception  provided by Allegro Hearth Bakery and Margaret's Fine Imports! 

Film Workshop with Director Petr Lom
Monday, April 15, 5:00pm -- Giant Eagle Auditorium, Baker Hall, Carnegie Mellon University


Faces of Media is excited to present a film workshop by Back to the Square director and Harvard-educated philosophy professor-turned-filmmaker Petr Lom!  Everyone from students to academics to artists will enjoy this class led by an individual with a profound appreciation for teaching as well as filmmaking.

Petr works in a largely observational, cinema verité style, and in this class he will pass on some of the main lessons he has learned over the course of his career. He will discuss the challenges and ethical dilemmas of creating a human rights documentary, in addition to methods for cultivating one’s skill as an observational filmmaker.

** This event is FREE and open to the public!**


Riveting films and fascinating events highlighted Week Two!

The festival's second week of films brought in great crowds to see Blood in the Mobile, Gaamer, Student Short Films, Fame High, Fragments of a Revolution, and Hello! How Are You?. 

A LAN party, a director appearance, and an interactive art installation piece were just a few of the events that accompanied last week's compelling set of films. Join us for a final round of the festival's all-encompassing approach to examining global media outlets through film and in our own lives.  We look forward to seeing you later this week!

Seven years since its inception, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival continues to celebrate the art of filmmaking and the themes that define our contemporary social landscape. Through the power of the independent film, the 2013 Carnegie Mellon Film Festival: Faces of Media brings the intricacies surrounding media to life. These films both move us and enrich us by helping us better comprehend the unique ways in which media outputs shape our modern world.
