Blood in the Mobile * (Denmark, 2012)
Wednesday, April 3, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


*A Goodman Faces of Work Film

Click here for more information. 
We love our cell phones and the selection between different models has never been bigger. However, the production of these phones has a dark side. Blood in the Mobile finds director Frank Poulsen, sometimes referred to as the Danish version of Michael Moore, journeying into eastern DR Congo to investigate the country’s illegal mining industry. In doing so, he makes eye-opening discoveries that connect the minerals used to produce our cell phones to the civil war in the Congo.

A discussion moderated by Roger Rouse, Professor of History at CMU, will follow the screening!

Gaamer  (Ukraine, 2012)
Thursday, April 4, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information. 
School, friends, and family do not rank high on Alexander's list of priorities. This young Ukrainian gamer has only one passion: Quake. The game's "harrowing landscapes and veiled abysses" shut out Alexander's chronic boredom and numb his pervasive dissatisfaction. Putting his skill and ambition to the test, Alexander plays his way to the world championship. Can a title bridge the gap between Alexander's virtual life and his real one? Is recognition the answer to his problems?

Followed by a discussion moderated by Ralph Vituccio, Instructor in the Entertainment Technology Center at CMU, and a LAN Party!

Film Workshop with Director Scott Hamilton Kennedy
Friday, April 5, 1:00pm -- George R. White Theatre, Point Park University


This course is a unique opportunity to gain insights from Oscar-nominated filmmaker Scott Hamilton Kennedy (director of festival film Fame High) as he dissects each part of the film production process.

The workshop session will cover the process of independent documentary production from start to finish, and aspiring filmmakers of all experience levels are welcome to attend. Scott will discuss the steps involved in taking an original idea and making it into a successful documentary, highlighting necessary components such as script, funding, pitching ideas, shooting/collecting elements for the film, post production, and distribution.

This event is FREE and open to the public!

Student Short Film Screening 
Friday, April 5, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information.
For the fourth consecutive year, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival will include a Student Short Film Screening.  This exciting opportunity is open to all undergraduate and graduate students at accredited institutions, and its aim is to gather perspectives from diverse student filmmakers and animators on issues surrounding the concept of ‘Media’.

Fame High*  (USA, 2012)
Saturday, April 6, 7:00pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


*A Goodman Faces of Work Film

Click here for more information. 
Everyone dreams of fame. From the nail-biting freshman auditions to the spectacular year-end performances, Fame High captures the in-class and at-home drama, competition, heartbreak, and triumph during one school year at the Los Angeles County High School for the Arts, also known as Fame High. Talented teenagers reach for their dreams of becoming actors, singers, dancers, and musicians. This unique hybrid film is a Robert Altman-esque, coming-of-age, documentary-musical which follows a group of novice freshman and seasoned seniors struggling to find their voice - not only in their art but in life.

Features a discussion with director Scott Hamilton Kennedy, a special showcase performance by students in Pittsburgh's Magnet School for the Creative and Performing Arts (CAPA), and a delicious reception compliments of Green Pepper, one of the best Korean restaurants in Pittsburgh!

Fragments of a Revolution (France, 2011)
Sunday, April 7, 2:00pm -- Melwood Screening Room, 477 Melwood Avenue


Click here for more information. 
In Fragments of a Revolution, an anonymous filmmaker living virtually in Iran tells the story of the 2009-2010 election protests by sharing with us his personal archives of YouTube videos, tweets, emails, and other brief clips of the chaos that authorities tried to shield from our eyes. Like Anonymous, we travel to the streets of Tehran through social media channels and return to reality with a new sense of what it means to be countless but to still count.

CMU MFA student Felipe Castelblanco will present an art installation piece in collaboration with Iranian writer Nima Dehghani!  There will also be a pre-screening reception courtesy of Conflict Kitchen and Margaret’s Fine Imports.

Hello! How Are You?  (Romania/Spain/Italy, 2011)
Sunday, April 7, 5:00pm -- Regent Square Theater, 1035 S. Braddock Avenue 


Click here for more information. 
A cheerful film with winning performances, Hello! How Are You? is the story of a long-married couple more in love with their fantasies than with each other. Frustrated with the tedium of their lives, Gabby and Gaby each stumble through a series of circumstances into the murky world of Internet chat rooms. Liberated from the banality of the everyday, Gabby and Gaby finally discover their soul mates online. Can their virtual relationships revive their physical one? Rebooting it will take more than a simple Control-Alt-Delete.

Followed by a discussion moderated by Andreea Deciu Ritivoi, Associate Professor of English at CMU!


Week One brought great films, events, and discussions!

From fascinating director talks to lively receptions, the festival's opening week provided engaging opportunities to experience the multifaceted effects and possibilities of mass media!  We were happy to welcome new and familiar faces to our screenings of The We and the I, Crocodile in the Yangtze, Suspiciously in Love, InContact, Tahrir: Liberation Square, and Sushi: The Global Catch.

See you later this week for another round of compelling films, insightful conversation, and tastings of international foods from establishments in our very own Pittsburgh!

Seven years since its inception, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival continues to celebrate the art of filmmaking and the themes that define our contemporary social landscape. Through the power of the independent film, the 2013 Carnegie Mellon Film Festival: Faces of Media brings the intricacies surrounding media to life. These films both move us and enrich us by helping us better comprehend the unique ways in which media outputs shape our modern world.
