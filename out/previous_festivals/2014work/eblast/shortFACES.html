2014 CMU International Film Festival: Faces of Work
presents...



Friday, March 28th at 7:15pm
Melwood Screening Room

First Prize is sponsored by the Media Arts Program at Robert Morris University

Second Prize is sponsored by The Art Institute of Pittsburgh,
including the Media Arts & Animation; Digital Filmmaking & Video Production;
and Visual Effects & Motion Graphics Departments

The Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival is excited for another year of fantastic short films!  As part of the festival's 5th annual shortFACES Film Competition, international directors have submitted an impressive selection of original pieces that center around the theme of work.  These short film submissions will be judged by a panel of experts, including:
  • Jim Daniels, Screenwriter and the Thomas Stockham Baker Professor of English at CMU
  • James Duesing, Video Artist and Professor of Art at CMU
  • Nicolas Hurt, President of the CMU Filmmaking Club
  • Julia Joy, a senior at the University of Pittsburgh pursuing a degree in Film Studies and Communication
  • Carolina Loyola-Garcia, Multidisciplinary Artist, Filmmaker, and Associate Professor of Media Arts at Robert Morris University
  • Andrew Maietta, Associate Professor of Digital Film & Video Production at The Art Institute of Pittsburgh
Each film runs a maximum of 30 minutes. There will be a First Prize, Second Prize, Third Prize, and Honorable Mention. A reception will be provided, and admission is free!

Click here for more information about this event.



Admission is FREE and open to the public!


The eighth edition of the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival, sponsored by the Humanities Center, is dedicated to the legacy of world-renowned filmmaker, psychologist, and Carnegie Mellon professor, Paul Goodman, and to his professional focus on the human challenges and achievements of diverse groups of workers worldwide.  Audiences will have the opportunity to explore “Faces of Work” through the Pittsburgh premiere screenings of new, distinctive, and award-winning international films and documentaries from Brazil, Chile, Peru, Sweden, Poland, Bulgaria, Croatia, Germany, Austria, France, Egypt, Chad, Rwanda, Iran, India, Israel, China, and the USA.  More than a screening experience, the Faces Festival will offer Q&A sessions with directors, artists, and academics, live musical performances, international food receptions, and more!
