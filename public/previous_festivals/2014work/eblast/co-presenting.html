2014 Carnegie Mellon International Film

is proud to co-present ... 
Watchtower   (Turkey, 2012)
Sunday, November 17 -- 8:00pm @ Regent Square Theater
Tuesday, November 19 -- 9:15pm @ Melwood Screening Room


Click here for more information.

Organized by Three Rivers Film Festival.
Watchtower is a beautifully shot, intimate film that follows the story of two people who are drawn to each other while seeking refuge from the world.  Nihat has isolated himself as a fire warden in an observation tower far out in the wilderness, while Seher has abandoned her university studies to live in a makeshift room at the rural bus station where she has taken a job as tour hostess. When their fates collide, the pair forms a companionship that gradually allows each to redevelop a sense of compassion.

Winner of the Best Director and Best Cinematography Awards at the 2012 Adana Golden Boll Film Festival!   

Inequality for All   (USA, 2013)
Monday, November 18 -- 6:15pm @ McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information,
or contact CMU English Professor
Kathy Newman at kn4@andrew.cmu.edu.

Organized by Carnegie Mellon University and the Make it Our UPMC Campaign.

**FREE and open to the public** 
Inequality for All is a compelling and engaging documentary that uncovers the vast increase in income inequality through a blend of facts, infographics, interviews, and profiles of families whose lives have been scarred by the new American economy.  Robert Reich, a UC Berkeley Professor of Public Policy and former Secretary of Labor under President Bill Clinton, narrates the film and passionately displays his lifelong work to push for a just economy.  

Winner of the U.S. Documentary Special Jury Award at the 2013 Sundance Film Festival!

A pre-screening reception with pizza will take place at 5:15pm.  Following the film, there will be a Q&A session with Robert Reich via Skype!

**Catch Robert Reich again in the upcoming "Faces of Work" film At Berkeley, directed by the legendary documentarian Frederick Wiseman!**


The eighth edition of the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival, sponsored by the Humanities Center, is dedicated to the legacy of world-renowned filmmaker, psychologist, and Carnegie Mellon professor, Paul Goodman , and to his professional focus on the human challenges and achievements of diverse groups of workers worldwide.  Audiences will have the opportunity to explore “Faces of Work” through the Pittsburgh premiere screenings of new, distinctive, and award-winning international films and documentaries from Brazil, Mexico, Peru, Sweden, Romania, Turkey, Poland, Bulgaria, Croatia, Germany, France, Egypt, Chad, Iran, India, Vietnam, China, Japan, Canada, and the USA.  More than a screening experience, the Faces Festival will offer Q&A sessions with directors, artists, and academics, live musical performances, international food receptions, and more!
