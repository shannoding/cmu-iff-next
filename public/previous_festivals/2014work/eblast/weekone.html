2014 CMU International Film Festival: Faces of Work

WEEK ONE FILMS!

All foreign language films are accompanied by English subtitles.
Walesa: Man of Hope  (Poland, 2013)
Directed by Andrzej Wajda
Thursday, March 20 -- 7:15pm @ Regent Square Theater, 1035 S. Braddock Avenue
Sunday, March 23 -- 4:30pm @ McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information.
In Walesa: Man of Hope, Andrzej Wajda probes the private life of Polish "Solidarity" Trade Union leader, Lech Walesa, and wrests from its essence the incredible transformation of a shipyard worker into a charismatic leader.

Danuta Walesa, the former First Lady of Poland and wife of Lech Walesa, will make a special guest appearance! The event will also feature a Polish Opening Night Reception provided by S&D Polish Deli, and a discussion with famed Polish writer and journalist Piotr Adamowicz.

Shipbreakers  (India/USA, 2013)
Directed by Paul Goodman, Ralph Vituccio & Tom Clancey
Friday, March 21, 7:15pm -- McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University 



Click here for more information. 
Directed by and dedicated to the legendary CMU Professor and filmmaker Paul Goodman, Shipbreakers is as artistic as it is eye opening.  The film immerses us into the brutally tedious yet widely unknown trade of shipbreaking.

There will be an insightful panel discussion featuring Director Ralph Vituccio, Post-Gazette Assistant Managing Editor Mark Roth, and University of Pittsburgh Professors Michael Goodhart and Roger Rouse! Also features a CMU Opening Night Reception courtesy of Coriander India Grill.

The Square (Egypt/USA, 2013)
Directed by Jehane Noujaim 
Saturday, March 22 -- 4:30pm @ McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information.
The Square contributes profound optimism, relatable human faces, and an informative and nuanced view of Egypt's Revolution through hand-held camera footage that intimately immerses us into the site of conflict.

Features a panel discussion with University of Pittsburgh Professors Mohammed Bamyeh, Amani Attia, and Roger Rouse, along with a food reception generously provided by the  Allegro Hearth Bakery!

Web Junkie (China/Israel/USA, 2013)
Directed by Shosh Shlam & Hilla Medalia 
Saturday, March 22 -- 7:15pm @ McConomy Auditorium, Carnegie Mellon University


Click here for more information.
Web Junkie takes us behind the barbed wire fences of a Beijing Military facility where incessant Internet use is treated as a disease and provides us with a unique account of the work that goes into "fixing" a human mind.

The event includes a post-screening Q&A with Director Shosh Shlam and a special message from Dr. Kimberly Young, Internet Addiction Expert!

The eighth edition of the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival, sponsored by the Humanities Center, is dedicated to the legacy of world-renowned filmmaker, psychologist, and Carnegie Mellon professor, Paul Goodman, and to his professional focus on the human challenges and achievements of diverse groups of workers worldwide.  Audiences will have the opportunity to explore “Faces of Work” through the Pittsburgh premiere screenings of new, distinctive, and award-winning international films and documentaries from Brazil, Mexico, Peru, Sweden, Romania, Turkey, Poland, Bulgaria, Croatia, Germany, France, Egypt, Chad, Iran, India, Vietnam, China, Japan, Canada, and the USA.  More than a screening experience, the Faces Festival will offer Q&A sessions with directors, artists, and academics, live musical performances, international food receptions, and more!
