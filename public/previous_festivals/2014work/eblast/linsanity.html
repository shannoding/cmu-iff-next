Find FREE parking at the Forbes Avenue Parking Lot and East Campus Garage!   

"[Linsanity is] a sports doc with abundant heart... an insider's perspective to one of the NBA's most memorable career starts." - The Hollywood Reporter

What do faith, ambition, and basketball have in common? For Jeremy Lin, NBA stardom didn't just happen overnight. Lin, the first American basketball player of Taiwanese descent, managed to achieve great success without the aid of any athletic scholarships or drafts upon graduating from Harvard University. In this inspiring story of perseverance, Lin proves that a player of only 6 foot 3 can make it to the top. Now a crucial member of the Houston Rockets, Lin attributes his ability to overcome obstacles to loving family support and devout Christian beliefs. In Linsanity, we watch as Lin shoots hoops and stays positive before going onto achieve NBA fame - a true "slam dunk" of a film.

Linsanity will be preceded by a showing of Dr. Paul Goodman's short film Crew: Rowing in an 8. Following the determined Cornell University Women's Crew team as they train for an important race, we learn about the collective and individual strength involved in the competitive sport of rowing.   

An exclusive Q+A with producer, Allen Lu, will take place after the screening - come learn what it was like to work with Jeremy Lin on the film!

The first 200 guests will be treated to a reception of delicious Taiwanese cuisine! Signed DVDs, posters, and t-shirts will be available for purchase.

The eighth edition of the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival, sponsored by the Humanities Center, is dedicated to the legacy of world-renowned filmmaker, psychologist, and Carnegie Mellon professor, Paul Goodman, and to his professional focus on the human challenges and achievements of diverse groups of workers worldwide.  Audiences will have the opportunity to explore “Faces of Work” through the Pittsburgh premiere screenings of new, distinctive, and award-winning international films and documentaries from Brazil, Mexico, Peru, Sweden, Romania, Turkey, Poland, Bulgaria, Croatia, Germany, France, Egypt, Chad, Iran, India, Vietnam, China, Japan, Canada, and the USA.  More than a screening experience, the Faces Festival will offer Q&A sessions with directors, artists, and academics, live musical performances, international food receptions, and more!
