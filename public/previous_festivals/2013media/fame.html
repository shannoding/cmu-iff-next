"Fame may be fleeting, but the kids in Fame High will stay with you." - LA Times

Everyone dreams of fame. From the nail-biting freshman auditions to the spectacular year-end performances, Fame High captures the in-class and at-home drama, competition, heartbreak, and triumph during one school year at the Los Angeles County High School for the Arts, also known as Fame High. The film also intimately explores the concerns, expectations, and support (or lack thereof) the parents have, and the sacrifices they make for their talented teenagers as these students passionately pursue their dreams of becoming actors, singers, dancers, and musicians. This unique hybrid film is a Robert Altman-esque, coming-of-age, documentary-musical which follows a group of novice freshman and seasoned seniors struggling to find their voice - not only in their art but in life.

Presented in conjunction with “Goodman Faces of Work”, the Conservatory of Performing Arts at Point Park University, and the Pittsburgh Creative and Performing Arts School (CAPA).

The event will feature a discussion with director Scott Hamilton Kennedy, a special showcase performance by students in the Pittsburgh Creative and Performing Arts School (CAPA), and a delicious food reception courtesy of Green Pepper, one of the best Korean restaurants in Pittsburgh!

Film Workshop with Director Scott Hamilton Kennedy
Friday, April 5, 1:00pm
@ George R. White Theatre, Point Park University

The Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival: Faces of Media is also excited to present a film workshop by Fame High director and Oscar-nominated filmmaker Scott Hamilton Kennedy!  Aspiring filmmakers of all experience levels are welcome to attend.

This course is a unique opportunity to gain insights from Scott   as he dissects each part of the film production process from start to finish.  Scott will discuss the steps involved in taking an original idea and making it into a successful documentary, highlighting necessary components such as script, funding, pitching ideas, shooting/collecting elements for the film, post production, and distribution.

** This event is FREE and open to the public!** 

Seven years since its inception, the Carnegie Mellon International Film Festival continues to celebrate the art of filmmaking and the themes that define our contemporary social landscape. Through the power of the independent film, the 2013 Carnegie Mellon Film Festival: Faces of Media brings the intricacies surrounding media to life. These films both move us and enrich us by helping us better comprehend the unique ways in which media outputs shape our modern world.
